# Initial PRD — Definition of Done

## Criteria

| # | Criterion | Description |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Vision is compelling and quotable** | The product vision can be read in 2–3 sentences and immediately conveys what the product is, who it's for, and why it matters. |
| 2 | **Problem is research-backed** | The problem statement cites specific evidence from the Research stage, not just Baseline assumptions. |
| 3 | **Personas are enriched** | User personas have been refined with research insights — not just copied from Baseline. |
| 4 | **Product principles exist** | 3–5 guiding principles are defined that will drive scope and design decisions. |
| 5 | **MVP scope is genuinely minimal** | The MVP contains only features essential to validate the core product concept. No "nice-to-haves" hiding in MVP. |
| 6 | **Feature list uses 8-column format** | The feature list follows the standard format: Building Block, Feature, Description, Value, Success Indicators, Priority, Prototype Notes, Open Questions. |
| 7 | **Every feature traces to a need** | Each feature in the list connects to a validated user need from Baseline or Research. |
| 8 | **Core flows are complete** | At least 3 user flows are documented with trigger, steps, success state, and error states. |
| 9 | **UX direction is defined** | Design principles, interaction model, and information architecture are documented. |
| 10 | **Prototype prompts are ready** | Both MVP and Future prototype prompts contain enough context to generate useful prototype specifications. MVP prompt builds only MVP features; Future prompt extends MVP. |
| 11 | **All 6 deliverables exist** | initial-prd.md, feature-list.md, core-user-flows.md, ux-design-direction.md, prototype-prompt-mvp.md, and prototype-prompt-future.md are created. |
| 12 | **Scope boundaries are explicit** | MVP, V2, V3, and Out of Scope are clearly defined — no ambiguous features. |

## Evaluation

- All 12 criteria must pass.
- Pay special attention to criterion 5 (MVP minimality) — this is the most common failure point.
